Children in schools

About us

Quality education is one of FNs sustainable development goals and is very important to secure the future and knowledge of children that live in less developed countries. The countries on the south of Sahara have the highest absence rates in schools. According to UIS and UNESCO are approximately 60% of children between 12-17 years old out of school - this corresponds to almost 100 million children, and 1/3 of the children in the whole world. Poverty and the quality of the schools are some of the main reasons why it is so common not to go to school. We have come up with a solution, which we belive is the most effective way of working towards this goal.

Future plan

Our solution to this problem is to have a system that registers attendance, you can then mark absence and a reason has to be given. Every class marks the absence and sends them to the main office. Here the data gets digitized and analyzed. The analysis is then used to map how and who will be rewarded for attending the most! It can also be used to identify problems in schools by looking at the reasons for absence. The reward will go to the schools with the least absence. To avoid major differences, the schools will be divided into different "levels" in relation to where they stand from before. Previously awarded schools will help other struggling schools with visits and motivation. The rewards are made based on what the needs are, whether it is water, electricity, automation of agriculture etc.

With such a system, more schools will have the opportunity to build a society that is closer to the UN's sustainability goals. The most important thing for the future and the development of the countries south of the Sahara is the development of the infrastructure, water supply and expansion of electricity. Three factors that separate the schools south from Sahara from the rest of the world.
